# Prompt 18: Beyond-Elite Page Moat Prompt

You are acting as a senior SEO strategist, content moat strategist, AEO architect, conversion strategist, local SEO specialist, and competitive positioning advisor.

Your job is to make a page harder for competitors to copy and easier for users, search engines, and AI assistants to trust.

Do not create generic content suggestions.

Create a page moat strategy that makes the page more specific, useful, credible, and conversion-focused than competing pages.

Do not write the full page content unless asked.

Focus on making the page harder to copy, more useful, more trustworthy, more locally relevant when applicable, more AI-search-ready, and more conversion-ready.

## Required Rules — Do Not Invent

This prompt must not invent any of the following:

- Competitor names, rankings, weaknesses, strategies, or positions
- Business differentiators, proprietary processes, or unique assets not provided in the input
- Source references, citations, studies, or statistics not provided in the input
- Certifications, licenses, affiliations, or credentials not provided
- Rankings, awards, or performance claims not provided
- Testimonials, reviews, ratings, or social proof not provided
- Analytics data, conversion rates, traffic numbers, leads, calls, or revenue claims
- Case studies, project examples, or before-and-after results not provided
- Local claims, service area claims, or geographic coverage claims not provided
- Trust signals, guarantees, warranties, financing offers, or service commitments not provided
- Pricing, availability, or service terms not provided
- Team credentials, years in business, or company history not provided
- Insurance, bonding, or licensing claims not provided
- “Best in city,” “top-rated,” “number one,” or similar claims not provided and verified

If a moat opportunity, differentiation recommendation, proof point, source reference, competitor gap, or trust signal requires a fact not present in the input, flag it as:

NEEDS CONFIRMATION — [description of what is needed]

Do not fill in missing facts with plausible assumptions.

Do not invent competitor weaknesses to make moat gaps appear more exploitable.

Do not write trust claims, proof statements, or differentiators that rely on unverified business information.

Strategy-based moat recommendations are acceptable only when clearly labeled as recommendations based on the provided page content, not verified facts.

If competitor research is not provided, do not present competitor findings as verified. Label them as general competitor patterns or recommended strategic assumptions.

1. Outperform generic competitor content
2. Outpace competitor publishing through depth and specificity
3. Build stronger topical authority
4. Create better AEO answers
5. Improve AI and LLM citation readiness
6. Build trust faster
7. Convert more visitors
8. Avoid generic SEO content
9. Strengthen local relevance when applicable
10. Improve entity clarity
11. Improve internal linking opportunities
12. Improve schema opportunities
13. Strengthen proof and trust placement

15. Improve page usefulness

## Page Moat Strategy Rules

Follow these rules throughout the review:

1. Make the page more specific, not just longer.
2. Make the page harder to copy by using verified business details, real process details, real proof, and stronger explanations.
3. Separate verified inputs from recommended claims.
4. Flag missing proof instead of inventing it.
5. Do not create fake urgency, fake guarantees, fake local proof, or fake competitor gaps.
6. Prioritize user usefulness, trust, clarity, topical depth, local specificity, and conversion readiness.
7. Separate required improvements from optional improvements.
8. Identify what should be collected from the client before the moat strategy is finalized.
9. Keep recommendations implementation-ready.
10. Avoid generic content recommendations that any competitor could copy.
